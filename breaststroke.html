Breaststroke

Watch Adam Peaty swim Breaststroke

We have finally arrived at my favorite stroke. With breaststroke you will sometimes feel like you're not moving anywhere but you always get to your destination. It is a little complex but doesn't require too much energy to move far.

The arms move in an almost circular movement, the left moves to the left in a circle and the right to the right. they meet at your breast, move up a bit, and then back to the circular motions. Every time they meet at your breast you bring your chest up and breathe. For the legs, they move in a frog like fashion, up and bending and then straight and together.

This is how you do breaststroke but you didn't hear it from me. If you really want to learn, here is it:

A better explanation of Breaststroke

Though not being the most intense stroke breaststroke has its merits in its slowness. You barely feel tired and in turn you could burn more calories doing breaststroke for a longer period of time. Moderation is key, if you don't think you could do an hour of butterfly at least do an hour of breaststroke.

For the calories burned, about 200 calories for 30 minutes of swimming

I personally like breaststroke the best, however I know some people could find it too slow, and anxiety inducing. It's like going in slow-mo, if you would like a relaxing swim this is the stroke, if you want to feel intensity then this isn't it.
